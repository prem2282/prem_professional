Cost Optimization and Token Budgeting

Finance does not care about your embedding model’s vibes. They care about cost per successful task—and whether your token budget is a plan or a surprise invoice.

Memo from the CFO review

Ask: “Why did GenAI spend 3× after launch?”

Answer that works: We moved from pilot (1k users, short prompts) to production (40k users, RAG + agents). Unit cost per resolved ticket dropped 18%; total spend rose because volume and context size rose faster than we capped budgets.

Answer that fails: “Tokens are expensive.”

Cost optimization is not shaving pennies on the flagship model—it is allocating tokens like capacity: who gets them, for what outcome, with what fallback when the budget is gone.

Unit economics that engineering should own

  • Cost per successful outcome — resolved ticket, approved draft, passed eval—not cost per request.
  • Input vs output ratio — RAG-heavy apps often burn input tokens; agents burn output on tool JSON.
  • Hidden multipliers — rerank calls, eval judges, nightly re-embeds, failed retries.
  • Chargeback dimensions — team, feature flag, customer tier, environment.

Token budgeting mechanics

Think in three envelopes:

  1. Per-request ceiling — hard cap on input+output; reject or truncate with policy.
  2. Per-tenant daily pool — SaaS fairness; premium tiers buy more pool.
  3. Per-feature allocation — agents get a higher ceiling than FAQ bot, with finance sign-off.

Expose remaining budget to the orchestrator so it can choose “cheap path” (cached FAQ) vs “expensive path” (multi-step agent).

Governance hooks

Align with FinOps: tag every call with tenant_id, feature_id, model_id. Weekly review top ten expensive traces—not averages. Pair with the observability post for dashboards finance can trust.
